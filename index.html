Why Choose Bonds Over GICs?

Bonds offer a compelling alternative to traditional Guaranteed Investment Certificates (GICs) for Canadian investors seeking predictable higher returns. Unlike GICs, bonds provide the opportunity for greater income and capital appreciation. This guide will help you understand the key differences and advantages of investing in bonds.

Key Advantages of Bonds

Potentially Higher Returns

Bonds offer often higher yields than GICs, potentially benefiting your investment growth.

Regular Income

Many bonds pay regular interest payments, creating a steady stream of income.

Liquidity

Bonds can be traded on secondary markets, offering more flexibility than GICs.

Diversification

Bonds can diversify your portfolio and reduce overall risk.

GICs vs. Bonds: A Detailed Comparison

Feature GICs Bonds
Risk Low (CDIC insured) Moderate (varies by issuer)
Returns Fixed, generally lower Potentially higher, subject to market fluctuation
Liquidity Limited (locked until maturity) Higher (can be sold on secondary markets)
Income Paid at maturity Regular interest payments
